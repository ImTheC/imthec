These words that I write capture a moment.

A snapshot in time, a glimpse of my mind.

With colors and shading, a painter’s creating,

An image to be explored with the eye.

With talent and skill, the creator’s will

A moment is captured with dye.

But upon the page, my world you can gauge,

By not what you see, but that which you read.

At the click of a button, that which you see,

Can be captured and stored digitally.

In the future retrieve, these memories,

By viewing them all on the screen.

But upon the page, my world is displayed,

Not by pixels in sync, but by how you now think.

From the lowest of A’s to the highest of C’s,

Tones can be made for the ear to be pleased.

Melodies and lyrics with tempo and beat,

A symphony of feeling you love to repeat.

But upon the page, my world is arranged,

by not what you hear, but which words do appear.

So as you read, look past what you see,

To the affects that happen inside.

Deepen the feeling, carefully revealing,

My thoughts no longer I hide.

What I see, feel, and breathe, no longer kept pent.

For these words that I write capture a moment.

Up upon the risers, the choir stands their ground, While crowds of nameless faces, anticipate their sound. Voices dance with music, flowing to my ears, Fades into the distance, all but disappears. My world ignites with longing, when you I chance to see. Your world of sweet contentment, separate from me.

Your hair of darkest night, with glistening moonlight, Stirs a furied hurricane, my heart cannot contain. Our worlds are two, what can I do, to bridge this canyon grand. Our hearts to bind, our love to find, together, hand in hand.

The echoes of applause, slap me across the face, The rising tide of people, of you I lose all trace. Like I saw a shooting star, I walk away in awe, Wishing someday soon, you'll see the love I saw.

I choose my way across the floor, to the exit sign. As I open up the door, I find your hand on mine. We've both tried to exit, it seems at the same place. My heart explodes inside me, from this innocent embrace. You turn to me and I look back, our eyes connecting true, Instantly a world is formed, from what had once been two.

"Oh, excuse me," you do say, I cling to every word, Certain with no doubt inside, "I love you," I've just heard. I step aside and you walk through, into the parking lot. Filling up with human hordes, you're soon a tiny dot. What we've had is swallowed whole, by this chaotic beast, With dripping tongue on our one world, it absently does feast.

Slowly life continues, I watch weeks drain away, I wish I would have spoken, more to you that day. Two worlds again we have, and I'm thinking of you, Longing for that moment, when our worlds are not two.

Someday all will see, those two worlds will be so And when that day comes, my love for you you'll know. For now I lay here dreaming, of when all that may be, Perhaps that time is now, you've sent something to me.

Eagerly I open it, my pulse is racing fast, Finally the moment, I've been waiting for at last. Like a viper striking fast, I quickly feel the sting, My world crumbles around, the invite to your wedding.
